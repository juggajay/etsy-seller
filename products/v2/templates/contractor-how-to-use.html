How to Use Your Contractor Invoice

Quick setup guide for contractors, tradespeople & builders

1 Getting Started
  • This PDF has two pages: an Invoice (page 2) and a Quote (page 3). Use whichever you need.
  • Open in Microsoft Edge, Adobe Acrobat Reader (free), or Chrome. All fields and auto-calculations work across viewers.
  • Click on any highlighted field to start typing. Blue-highlighted fields are editable; grey fields calculate automatically.
  • To add your logo: in Adobe Acrobat, go to Edit PDF → Add Image and position over the “Your Logo” area.
2 Filling In Your Invoice
  • Job Details: Enter the work order number, job date, and expected completion date.
  • License / Reg #: Include your contractor license or trade registration number for compliance.
  • Job Site Address: Specify the work location if different from the client’s billing address.
  • Labour: List each task with hours and hourly rate. Amount auto-calculates (Hours × Rate).
  • Materials: List parts, supplies, and materials with quantity and unit cost. Amount auto-calculates.
  • Materials Markup: Default is 15%. Change the percentage — the markup amount recalculates automatically.
  • Call-out Fee: Add a fixed service call or site visit fee if applicable.
  • Tax: Default is 10%. Change the rate if needed — tax amount recalculates automatically.
  • Deposit: Enter any deposit received. Balance Due auto-calculates (Total − Deposit).
3 Using the Quote Page
  • Page 3 is a matching Quote template for job estimates, proposals, and pre-approvals.
  • Break down labour and materials so clients see the full cost structure before approving.
  • Quote totals include tax and markup. No deposit fields (those apply to invoices only).
  • Set the validity period (default: 30 days) and include warranty terms upfront.
Contractor Tips

Save a blank copy: Always keep an unedited version as your master template.
Materials markup: Industry standard is 15–25%. Adjust based on your trade and supply costs.
Warranty: Specify labour warranty period separately from manufacturer parts warranty.
File → Save As: Save each completed invoice with a unique name (e.g., INV-001-Smith-Kitchen.pdf).
