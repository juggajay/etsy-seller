How to Use Your Freelancer Invoice

Quick setup guide for freelancers and consultants

1 Getting Started
  • This PDF has two pages: an Invoice (page 2) and a Quote (page 3). Use whichever you need.
  • Open in Microsoft Edge, Adobe Acrobat Reader (free), or Chrome. All fields and auto-calculations work across viewers.
  • Click on any highlighted field to start typing. Blue-highlighted fields are editable; grey fields calculate automatically.
  • To add your logo: in Adobe Acrobat, go to Edit PDF → Add Image and position over the “Your Logo” area.
2 Filling In Your Invoice
  • Project Details: Enter the project name, client PO/reference number, and payment terms (e.g., Net 30).
  • Tasks Section: Enter each task or deliverable, hours spent, and your hourly rate. The Amount column auto-calculates (hours × rate).
  • Tax: Default is 10%. Change the rate if needed — tax amount recalculates automatically.
  • Deposit: If a deposit was paid upfront, enter the amount. The Balance Due auto-calculates (total − deposit).
  • Late Fee: Default is 1.5% per month. Adjust to match your terms or set to 0 if not applicable.
  • Due Date: Set based on your payment terms. For Net 30: invoice date + 30 days.
3 Using the Quote Page
  • Page 3 is a matching Quote template for project proposals.
  • Itemise your scope so clients see the value before they commit.
  • Quote totals include tax. No deposit or late fee fields (those apply to invoices only).
  • Set the validity period (default: 30 days) and describe the full scope of work in the footer.
Freelancer Tips

Save a blank copy: Always keep an unedited version as your master template.
Payment terms: Net 30 is standard. Use Net 15 for new clients or smaller projects.
Deposits: Request 50% upfront for projects over $1,000 to protect your cash flow.
File → Save As: Save each completed invoice with a unique name (e.g., INV-001-ClientName.pdf).
