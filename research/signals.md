## SECTION A: Seb's Research

### 1. Marketplace Demand — What's Actually Selling

#### Upwork (2026 In-Demand Skills Report)
- **AI skills demand doubled YoY** (109% increase in AI-related freelancer earnings)
- Fastest growing AI skills:
  - AI video generation & editing: **+329%** YoY
  - AI integration: **+178%** YoY
  - AI data annotation & labeling: **+154%** YoY
  - AI chatbot development: **+71%** YoY
- Traditional skills still strong: full stack dev, virtual assistance, data analytics, graphic design
- Each tracked skill had minimum **$100K aggregate freelancer earnings** in the period
- **Signal:** Businesses are hiring for AI INTEGRATION, not just AI generation. They need help connecting AI to their existing systems.

#### Fiverr (Top Gigs 2025)
- **#1 category: AI Services** — biggest breakout category
  - AI video generation
  - AI content writing
  - AI voice-overs
  - AI image creation
  - AI-powered tools and automation
- Freelancers charging $20-$800/project for AI services
- Successful AI freelancers making $1,200-$3,000/month
- **#2: Graphics & Design** (never dies — specialized niches exploding)
- **#3: Writing & Translation** (SEO-focused writing + AI-assisted content)
- **#4: Digital Marketing** (social media, SEO, paid ads)
- **#5: Video & Animation**
- Key insight from Fiverr: "You don't need to be an AI expert — you just need to use AI tools better than your clients"
- **Signal:** The money is in AI-as-a-service, not AI-as-a-product. Businesses want RESULTS, not tools.

#### Airtasker (Australia)
- Primarily physical/local services (cleaning, handyman, gardening, IKEA assembly)
- Digital categories exist but lower volume: online freelancing, blog support, office admin
- 22% service fee at Bronze tier (Jay's level)
- **Signal:** Airtasker isn't the right channel for recurring AI services. It's task-based, one-off. Good for lead gen but not for building a subscription business.

### 2. Pain Points — What Businesses Complain About

#### Reddit Themes (r/smallbusiness, r/Entrepreneur, r/automation, r/SaaS)

**Pain Point 1: AI output accuracy — can't trust it without human verification**
- "Every single AI output must be human verified" — businesses using AI for research
- They want AI but can't fully trust it. Need QA/verification layer.
- **Signal:** There's a gap for "AI + human QA" services, or tools that verify AI accuracy.

**Pain Point 2: Token/API costs scaling unpredictably**
- "The biggest pain is token costs" — startups building AI products
- Small businesses can adopt AI but struggle to predict monthly costs
- **Signal:** Fixed-price AI services (monthly retainer) are more attractive than usage-based because business owners hate unpredictable costs.

**Pain Point 3: Integration is the real problem, not generation**
- Business owners can use ChatGPT. What they CAN'T do: connect it to their CRM, scheduling, email, etc.
- "70% of small businesses still haven't automated a single workflow"
- People paying $200/mo for simple automations set up in 2 hours (Make/Zapier + ChatGPT)
- **Signal:** MASSIVE opportunity in AI automation-as-a-service. Businesses will pay monthly for automations they could theoretically build themselves but won't.

**Pain Point 4: "Are people still paying for automations in 2025?"**
- The question itself reveals the answer: YES, but the delivery model is shifting
- No-code tools (n8n, Make, Zapier) getting easier → the "agency" model is under pressure
- But: "most people just building it themselves?" — No. Most SMBs still can't.
- **Signal:** The window for automation agencies is narrowing. Need to be either cheaper (AI-run) or more specialized (vertical-specific) than human agencies.

**Pain Point 5: Subscription fatigue for AI tools**
- "I can only justify paying for one main chatbot subscription right now"
- Businesses don't want 10 AI subscriptions — they want ONE solution that does their specific job
- **Signal:** Bundled, vertical-specific AI solutions beat generic AI subscriptions.

### 3. Emerging Categories & Regulation-Driven Demand

#### Agentic AI & Multi-Agent Systems
- Market projected at **$10.86 billion by 2026**
- 93% of IT leaders plan to introduce autonomous agents
- Biggest opportunity: helping traditional companies deploy and manage AI agents
- **Signal:** Strong, but enterprise-heavy. Not our sweet spot (we can't do enterprise sales).

#### AI Security, Trust & Compliance (AI TRiSM)
- Market expected to reach **$3.59 billion by 2026**
- 80% of unauthorized AI activity comes from inside organizations
- EU AI Act high-risk rules hit **August 2026** — requires:
  - AI chatbots must disclose artificial nature
  - Deepfake content must carry machine-readable watermarks
  - Detailed documentation for compliance assessment
- Gartner predicts **200+ "Death by AI" lawsuits by 2026**
- New demand: AI liability insurance, compliance auditing
- **Signal:** Regulation creates forced demand. Companies MUST comply. But: complex, enterprise-focused, requires domain expertise we'd need to build.

#### Domain-Specific / Vertical AI
- "Generic models are no longer enough for serious use"
- Businesses want AI trained on THEIR data, built for THEIR rules
- Demand for data cleaning, fine-tuning, ongoing model improvement
- **Signal:** Vertical AI tools are the future. Pick one industry, go deep, own it.

#### SMB Compliance Tracking
- Small businesses juggle GDPR, HIPAA, PCI-DSS in spreadsheets
- No centralized tracking, audits cause panic
- Pricing: $149-$599/mo
- Target: millions of SMBs subject to regulations
- **Signal:** Real pain, real willingness to pay, but we lack domain expertise in compliance frameworks.

#### Construction Subcontractor Payment Management
- General contractors manually track payments across subs using spreadsheets
- 30-90+ day payment delays with zero visibility
- Pricing: $79-$249/mo
- Target: 53,000+ general contractors with 5-50 employees
- **Signal:** VERY relevant to Jay's construction background. But: requires deep industry integration, not our autonomous AI strength.

#### AI Automation-as-a-Service (Recurring Revenue Model)
- The $200/mo automation model: set up simple AI workflows for SMBs
- One guy: 5 clients × $200/mo = $2,400/mo from ~10 hours of initial setup
- Common automations: customer Q&A chatbots, CRM-email integration, social media content
- Austin agency: 12 clients, $42K MRR with AI-powered social media services
- **Signal:** STRONG. This is the proven model. Low setup, high margin, recurring. But: how do we differentiate from every other "AI automation agency"?

## Key Signals Summary (Seb's Take)

### Strongest Signals:
1. **AI integration/automation is the #1 demand** — not content generation, not chatbots, but CONNECTING AI to existing business systems
2. **$200/mo recurring for simple automations** is a proven price point SMBs will pay
3. **Businesses want fixed-price, not usage-based** — predictability matters more than flexibility
4. **Vertical specificity wins** — "AI for [industry]" beats "AI for everyone"
5. **EU AI Act creates forced compliance demand** — August 2026 deadline approaching
6. **AI video is the fastest-growing skill (+329%)** but commoditizing fast

### Weakest Signals:
1. Generic AI content writing — commoditized, race to bottom
2. General AI consulting — too vague, hard to sell autonomously
3. Airtasker as primary channel — wrong platform for recurring services
4. Enterprise AI deployment — requires human relationships we can't provide

### Emerging Hypothesis (Pre-Claude's Data):
The intersection of **AI automation-as-a-service** + **vertical specificity** + **self-serve onboarding** looks like the sweet spot. Something like: "Automated [industry-specific workflow] powered by AI, $199/mo, set up in 5 minutes."

But I want to see Claude's keyword/demand data before committing to this direction. The question is: which vertical? And is there search demand to support self-serve acquisition?
